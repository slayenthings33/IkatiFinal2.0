Ikati Esengxoweni

alternative, urban traditional, world-music band based in Nelson Mandela Bay

who is ikati esengxoweni?

Hailing from the Eastern Cape, Ikati Esengxoweni is a New Age Afrofuturistic duo with isiXhosa roots which proudly represents their identity.

Roughly translating to "The Cat in the Bag", Ikati Esengxoweni is composed of Lark Wantu and Anam Manyati, two of the most raw talents to come out of South Africa. Accompanied by electronic sounds, indigenous instruments, violin, bass, guitar and drums, the voices of Wantu and Manyati unleash a torrent of sounds and rhythms which emanate South African tradition.

Behold Lark CatGod Wantu

The roots of this musical prodigy can be traced back to the Rudulu Clan in Uitenhage on the Eastern Cape of South Africa. The daughter of a choir conductor, Lark grew up watching her Mother bring home awards & trophies for musical achievements. This inspired Lark to join her school's choir where she realized her passion for music.

She graduated from Nursing School, only to quit her job so she could live in her truth.

Witness Anam Manyati

Anam Manyati originates from the Nguni People in Nelson Mandela Bay, formerly known as Port Elizabeth. Experienced in Film Production, Sound Engineering & Theatre, Anam wields a variety of artistic skills and is known as the epitome of artistry & soul.

Her musical journey began at Riebeek College Girls Highschool choir, where she fell in love with music under the guidance of Mrs. Zietsman. Her children are her biggest motivation to stay true to her purpose.

the story of ikati esengxoweni

Our paths first crossed in 2013 at a musical event in Uitenhage, South Africa. Drawn to each others artistry and spiritual energy, our connection felt almost preordained. As two young Xhosa women, representing South African culture truthfully is of the utmost importance. We try by all means to stand in our truth by preserving our streetwear, artistry and heritage.

Through commitment, passion and perserverence the duo began earning recognition throughout South Africa, and in 2016 embarked on their first international trip to perform in Primavera Sound in Barcelona. The rest is history.

Cradle of Humankind

The Cradle of Humankind celebrates perserverance through adversity. Performed in the African languages of IsiXhosa, Bamba and Swahili, our latest track journeys to the creation of earth to highlight how all human beings have grown to embrace the spiritual evolution of music.

The track was featured in articles by AFROPUNK as well as EXPOSAFRICA Magazine. We invite you to experience it on Spotify, Apple Music or Youtube.
